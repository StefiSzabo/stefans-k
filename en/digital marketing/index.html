What is Marketing?

Many people understand Marketing as the promotion and advertising of products and services, which is not correct, but also not wrong.
Marketing is more than that, it is an extensive process of analyzing a company's customers, the market and the environment in which it operates.
It is the process of creating, delivering and exchanging value for customers, clients, partners and society at large.
It involves understanding the customer needs and wants, developing products or services that satisfy those needs and wants,
communicating the value proposition of those products or services and distributing them to the target market.

Marketing includes all those actions of selling, promoting, attracting and mantaining customers, demand, how products and services are offered,
connecting the company in relation to the market in which it is located, defining the clientele, analyzing the behavior of consumers and customers, the connection of the company with her clients.
Marketing also involves managing customer relationships and measuring the effectiveness of Marketing activities.

Marketing is said to be the voice of the customer to the company, but also the voice of the company to the customers and the market. It is also called art.
The Marketing department works directly with the Sales department and sometimes it is the one and the same department.

What is Digital Marketing?

Digital Marketing is that form of Marketing, which includes all the actions mentioned above, happening in the digital environment.
It uses the internet and digital technologies, such as computers, smartphones and AI, to connect with customers.

Digital Marketing includes various channels and methods, such as e-mail, social media, advertising and multimedia messaging.
A Digital marketer will usually deal with online advertising, mailing lists and e-mails, online sales and subscriptions,
analyzing the company's web pages and social media accounts, conversion rates (sale, website entry etc.) and click rates,
blog posts and company's social media posts, SEO, SEM, etc.

Digital Marketing also allows marketers to collect data, track customer behavior and tailor content to specific audiences.
He/She, according to the function and the job he/she has, will always use platforms such as the platforms of the company's social media accounts and the analysis platform of each account.
(e.g. for Facebook and Instagram is Meta Business Suite), analysis platforms such as Google Analytics and Google Search Console,
ad running platforms, like Meta Business Suite, WhatsApp Business and Google Ads etc.

Digital Marketing also enables marketers to reach customers across different devices and platforms.

Marketing vs Digital Marketing

Some of the main differences between Marketing and Digital Marketing are:

1. Offline and Online

Traditional Marketing includes offline activities, such as print, radio and TV.
Digital Marketing is focused on online and digital activities, such as e-mail, social media, advertising and multimedia messaging.

2. The audience

Marketing can reach a broad and diverse audience, but it can be difficult to target specific segments and measure the impact.
Digital Marketing can reach a niche and targeted audience, but it can be challenging to stand out from the competition and attract attention.

3. The techniques

Marketing can rely on traditional and established techniques, such as the 4 P's (Product, Price, Place and Promotion)
Digital Marketing, besides those, can require inovative and experimental techniques, such as the 4 C's (Consumer, Cost, Convenience and Communication)

4. The approach

Marketing can have a long-term and strategic approach, such as building brand awarreness and loyalty.
Digital Marketing can have a short-term and tactical approach, such as generating leads and conversions.

Advantages and Disadvantages

\ Marketing Digital Marketing
Advantages

  • It can reach a large and diverse audience,
    which can increase brand awareness and recognition.

  • It can use various media and formats,
    which can appeal to different senses and emotions.

  • It can build trust and credibility,
    which can enhance customer loyalty and retention.

  • It can be more cost-effective and efficient,
    which can increase profitability and return on investment.

  • It can be measurable and accountable,
    which can enable the ability to optimize and improve
    Digital Marketing performance.

  • It can be more interactive and engaging,
    which can enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty.
Disadvantages

  • It can be costly and resource-intensive,
    which can reduce profitability and return on investment.

  • It can be difficult to measure and evaluate,
    which can limit the ability to optimize and improve
    Marketing performance.

  • It can be affected by external factors,
    such as regulations, competitors and environmental changes,
    which can reduce the effectiveness and relevance of
    Marketing messages.

  • It can be competitive and saturated,
    which can reduce the visibility and differentiation of
    Digital Marketing messages.

  • It can be more complex and dynamic,
    which can require multiple skills and tools
    to manage Digital Marketing activities.

  • It can be more risky and vulnerable,
    which can expose the brand to negative feedback
    and reputation damage.
