K-Pop culture refers to the popular music and lifestyle of South Korea, which has gained global recognition and influence in recent decades.
It encompasses not only the music itself, but also the fashion, beauty, dance, and fandom aspects of it.
The main features of the K-Pop culture are:

(If you meet terms that you don't know what they mean, you can find their definition after this)

1. The music

The K-Pop music is characterized by catchy hooks, polished choreography, grandiose live performances, and impeccably produced music videos.
It is influenced by various genres, such as Pop, Hip-Hop, R&B, Rap, Rock, EDM, as well as traditional Korean elements.
It often incorporates English words or phrases, have English version or the song, or have full English songs to appeal to international audiences.
You can find some of the most popular K-Pop groups at the "Tops" section of this page.

2. The fashion

The K-Pop fashion is known for its colorful, trendy, and sometimes eccentric styles.
The idols often wear outfits that reflect their musical concepts or personalities, raging from cute and casual to chic and glamorous.
The K-Pop fashion also influences the street style and online shopping trends of many fans around the world.

3. The beauty

The K-Pop beauty is based on the ideal of having flawless skin, natural makeup, attractive features, and following the KBS.
K-Pop idols often endorse or collaborate with various cosmetic brands.
This beauty also inspires many fans to adopt skincare routines, try new products, or experiment with different looks.

4. The dance

The K-Pop dance is an integral part of the music and performance of K-Pop idols.
It involves complex and synchronized movements that showcase the skills and charisma of the idols.
It also attracts many fans who enjoy learning the choreography or creating their own covers of their favorite songs.

5. The fandom

The K-Pop fandom is one of the most passionate and loyal communities in the world.
The fans support their favorite idols by buying their albums and merchandise, streaming their songs and videos,
voting for them in awards shows, attending their concerts and fan meetings, and joining online fan clubs or forums.
The K-Pop fans also express their creativity and love for their idol by making fan art, fan fiction, fan videos, or fan projects.

The K-Pop culture is a phenomenon that reflects the diversity and dynamism of South Korea and its people.
It also connects millions of fans across different countries and cultures through a shared appreciation and enjoyment of music and entertainment.

K-Pop terms that you will hear a lot

1. K-Pop Stans

The word stan is a portmanteau of stalker and fan, and it originated from the 2000 song "Stan" by Eminem,
which tells the story of an obsessed fan who writes letters to his idol and eventually kills himself and his pregnant girlfriend.
The word stan has been since adopted by various fandoms to describe faans who are extremely devoted, passionate, and sometimes obsessive about their faavorite celebrities or groups.
They are fans who follow and support one or more K-Pop artists or groups, often on social media platforms such as Twitter, Instagram or YouTube.
K-Pop Stans are known for their ability to mobilize and coordinate online campaigns, such as streaming songs and videos, voting for awards, trending hashtags, or sabotaging opponents.
They are also involved in various social and political causes, such as supporting the Black Lives matter movement, disrupting white supremacist hashtags, or pranking Donald Trump's rally.
However, some K-Pop stans may also engage in toxic or problematic behaviors, such as cyberbullying, harassment, or fan wars with other fandoms.

2. Kpoppers

The word kpopper is a combination of K-Pop and popper, which is a slang term for a person who likes pop music.
Kpoppers are fans who enjoy listening to K-Pop music and appreciate the diversity and quality of the genre.
They may not be as invested or involved in the fandom activities as K-Pop Stans, but they still respect and support the artists and groups they like.
Kpoppers may also be more open-minded and tolerant of different opinions and preferences than some K-Pop Stans.
They may like one or more K-Pop artists or groups, or they may like K-pop music in general.

Entertainment companies

According to the latest survey, there were a total of 4.610 companies in South Korean entertainment industry as of 2020.
Among them, almost 2.800 were management agencies and 1.800 were production agencies.
However, the number of entertainment agencies may vary depending on the definition and scope of the industry.

The most successful and influential entertainment agencies in South Kore are often referred to as the Big 4, which consist of HYBE, JYP, SM and YG.
These four companies have produced some of the most popular and internationally acclaimed K-Pop groups.
As of February 2023, the market capitalization of these four companies ranged from 1.3 trillion to 6.9 trillion South Korean won.
